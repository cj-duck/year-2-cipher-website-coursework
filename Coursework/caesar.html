Caesar Cipher

The Caesar cipher cipher is named after Julius Caesar who used it (with a key of 3) to protext messages of military significance. Although Caesar's was the first recorded use of this ciper there are other substitution ciphers known to have been used earlier. Read more here.

The cipher works by replacing a letter with another letter x number of places after or before it in the alphabet as shown by the diagram to the left. The number of places a letter is shifted by is known as the key. This cipher is not very secure as it would take a maximum 25 attempts to crack the message.
